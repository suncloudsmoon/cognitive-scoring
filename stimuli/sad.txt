The house felt unbearably quiet after the funeral. She sat alone in the empty kitchen, staring at the chair where he used to sit every morning. The silence was heavier than any sound she had ever known.

He walked through the abandoned neighborhood where he grew up. The playground was rusted and overgrown. Every broken window reminded him of friends who had moved away and would never return.

The rain fell steadily as she packed the last of her belongings into cardboard boxes. This had been their home for twenty years, and now it was just walls and empty rooms. She whispered goodbye to no one.

The old man sat on the park bench feeding pigeons, the same bench where his wife used to sit beside him. He came every day at the same time, hoping the routine would fill the emptiness inside him. It never did.

She read the letter one more time before folding it carefully. The words were kind but final. The relationship was over, and there was nothing left to say. She turned off the light and lay in the darkness.
